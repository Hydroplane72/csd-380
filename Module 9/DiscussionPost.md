# Describe an improvement blitz. How many participants should be included? How long does it last?

## Description
An improvement blitz is generally defined as a short, intense, and focused period during which a team works on a specific process, workflow, and/or problem and aims to deliver measurable improvements quickly. 3 main things define an improvement blitz and make it an actual blitz, not just a focus period. 

1. The scope of change is narrow
2. The timeline to complete is small
3. The team is focused on this specific change

## How many people?
Generally, there are 6-12 people with different roles.

Number of People - Description of them
1-2 are process owners (stakeholders)
2-4 processors (People who actually use the process and/or do the work)
1-3 other process owners that could be affected (Stakeholders of other processes)
1 The person who keeps the process moving

Generally, when you have fewer than 6 people, you don't have enough people in the room to make decisions. On the other hand, when you have more than 12, it can slow down decision-making since you have to wait for agreement from more people.

## Length of time?
This normally lasts between 1 and 3 days. If it takes more than 3 days, it should be considered a full project rather than a blitz.

## My thoughts
While this sounds great, in reality, you rarely have the right people with the authority to make decisions in the room with you. Because of this, you can say decisions were made, but in reality, the decisions are final until someone random outside the group decides they have an opinion. This method potentially works for some companies; the example of my work is not one of them, however much I wish it were.
